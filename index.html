The Environmental Impact of Computing: A Research Report

Analyzing how computer science both supports climate mitigation and contributes to global energy demands.

Our research examines the complex, bidirectional relationship between the computing industry and the environment [1]. While digital tools like climate modeling and logistics algorithms help reduce carbon emissions, the technology itself accounts for over 2% of global energy demand [2].

This website presents our group's findings on these intersecting roles [3]. We aim to provide students and professionals with a clear look at how technology can be a solution to the climate crisis—and where it remains a significant part of the problem [4].

What's Inside

Our findings are organized into four key areas of the computer science and climate relationship [5]:

  • Mitigation Strategies: Data on how sensors, predictive modeling, and AI optimize energy and monitor environmental changes [6]. Learn more.
  • Industry Footprint: Research into the energy consumption of data centers, AI training, and hardware manufacturing [7]. Learn more.
  • Information Risks: How AI-generated misinformation can undermine scientific consensus and delay climate action [8]. Learn more.
  • Project Background: Information about our research group and our commitment to open-access data. Learn more.

Our Research Mission

This project was developed by a research team at Conestoga College to investigate the link between computer science and climate change [9]. Our goal is to highlight that while computing is essential for environmental monitoring, the industry must address its own rising carbon footprint [10].
