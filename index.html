Computing for a Sustainable Future

How computer science both helps and harms our climate—and what we can do about it.

Computer science is at the heart of the climate conversation. On one hand, it gives us powerful tools to monitor our environment, predict extreme weather, and optimize energy use. On the other hand, the technology we rely on—data centers, AI systems, and digital devices—consumes significant energy and produces waste.

This website explores the bidirectional relationship between computing technology and climate change. Our goal is to help researchers, students, and industry professionals understand both how technology can be part of the solution—and how it can sometimes make the problem worse.

Monitoring the Environment

Digital devices and sensors collect real-time data on air quality, temperature, and environmental changes—giving us an accurate picture of climate change as it happens.

Climate Modeling & Prediction

Advanced simulations help scientists predict extreme weather events and long-term climate patterns, enabling better preparation and policy decisions.

Energy Optimization

AI and algorithms optimize energy use in transportation, logistics, and power grids—reducing waste and carbon emissions across industries.

The Challenge Ahead

Computing technology itself has an environmental cost. Data centers, AI systems, and hardware production consume energy and create e-waste—and misinformation about climate change spreads faster than ever.

What You Will Find Here

This resource explores the multifaceted effects of computing technology on our climate. Whether you're a researcher, student, or industry professional, you'll find insights into both sides of this complex relationship.

  • Pros: How computer science helps mitigate climate change—through environmental monitoring, climate modeling, energy optimization, and innovation in renewable technologies.
  • Cons: Understanding the negative impacts—the energy consumed by data centers, the e-waste from hardware, and the spread of climate misinformation through AI.
  • Ethics & Intellectual Property: Exploring responsible communication, the legal frameworks protecting technical innovations, and why we chose a Creative Commons license.
  • Our Team: Meet the research group behind this project.
