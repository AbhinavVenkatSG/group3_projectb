Hi, 

Our team has drafted an outline of content for the proposed informational website about the bidirectional relationship between computer science and climate change.  Below is a brief overview of the main topics we expect to cover.  We would greatly appreciate your feedback. 



 

How Computer Science Helps Mitigate and Address Climate Change 

Digital devices are used to monitor and collect data from the environment, giving us an accurate view of climate change as it occurs. 

Computing techniques are used to perform complex modeling/simulations of the climate and weather systems.  This allows us to predict extreme weather as well as long-term climate patterns. 

Computer systems, including artificial intelligence (AI), can be used to optimize energy usage. 

For example, transportation and logistics optimization algorithms help to decrease fuel consumption through the calculation of the most efficient paths, which decreases carbon emissions in supply chains. 

Computer systems can aid innovation to tackle climate change, for example with renewable energy and smart grid technologies. 

Researchers are making progress on green computing, which focuses on energy-efficient hardware and software.  This offers practical solutions to reduce the environmental impact of technology. 

 

How Computing Exacerbates Climate Change 

The information and communications technology industry accounts for more than 2% of global energy demand, which is expected to increase.  Much of the energy comes from carbon-emitting sources, which contribute to climate change.  Examples of how the computing industry consumes energy: 

Construction and operation of data centers 

Crypto mining, cloud services, and AI 

Production of digital device hardware 

AI-powered misinformation and disinformation can be used to undermine the scientific consensus on climate change, potentially delaying action.
