Data & Statistics

Evidence-based climate trends and the quantitative role of computing in decarbonization.

To effectively combat climate change, we must first measure it with precision. The following data highlights the accelerating trends in global warming and identifies industrial sectors where computer science solutions offer the highest potential for impact.

Global Temperature Trends

Data from global meteorological agencies shows a clear upward trajectory in temperature anomalies over the last century. Computer models use this historical data to predict future shifts under various CO2 concentration scenarios.

Decade Avg. Temperature Anomaly (°C) Notable Climatic Event
1981–1990 +0.26 Montreal Protocol implementation
1991–2000 +0.40 Warmest decade of the 20th century
2001–2010 +0.62 Rapid Arctic sea ice decline begins
2011–2020 +0.98 Paris Agreement drafted (2015)

Source: NASA Goddard Institute for Space Studies (GISS).

Key Data Insight

Advanced computing can help reduce global CO2 emissions by up to 20% by 2030 through systematic optimizations in energy, logistics, and industrial processes. High-fidelity carbon tracking software is now the primary tool used by corporations to adhere to international sustainability standards.

Role of Computing in Carbon Tracking

Carbon footprint tracking has evolved from rough estimates to real-time, sensor-driven accounting. Cloud-based platforms now integrate data from manufacturing floors, shipping fleets, and office facilities to provide a comprehensive view of an organization's environmental impact. This granular data is essential for verified carbon offsets and regulatory compliance.
